ℹ︎ Parameters & growth models explained

Growth models — how FPV (network size) grows over time

Decaying — Growth starts fast and only slows down. The quarter-over-quarter (QoQ) rate begins at your Initial QoQ growth and decays smoothly toward the Long-term growth floor. Models a launch with strong early adoption that matures. gᵢ = floor + (g−floor)·e^(−0.45(i−1))

Peak & settle — Growth accelerates first, peaks around Q4–Q5, then decelerates to the floor by ~Q13. The QoQ rate follows a gamma-shaped hump rather than a straight decline. Models an adoption S-curve: slow start → acceleration → maturity. Starts at the same initial rate but climbs above it before falling.

Wasabi — A real-world benchmark using Wasabi (cloud-storage startup) QoQ rates: ~32%/q year 1, 14%/q year 2, 10%/q year 3, 4%/q after. The growth-rate sliders are ignored; only the starting FPV applies.

Custom — Set the QoQ growth rate for each quarter Q3–Q16 yourself (pre-filled with the decaying path; type in Molly's per-quarter numbers). Quarters beyond Q16 grow at the Long-term rate.

1 · Network size (FPV)

Starting FPV(Q2) — Filecoin Pay Volume in the first measured quarter (USD). FPV = total $ value of deal payments settled through Filecoin Pay in a quarter — not the fee. Q1 is a bootstrap quarter with no measured volume. This seeds the network size.

Initial QoQ growth (Q2→Q3) — the starting quarter-over-quarter FPV growth rate (Decaying & Peak models).

Long-term growth — the steady-state QoQ rate the trajectory settles toward (Decaying & Peak models).

PDP data stored @Q2 (FOC only) — total data under PDP / Filecoin-Onchain-Cloud storage (TiB) in the first measured quarter; grows along the FPV trajectory. Drives the proof-fee burn. This is the PDP network, not the whole Filecoin network — the 0.00023 FIL/TiB fee is charged only on PDPVerifier provePossession, never on legacy sealed-sector storage (WindowPoSt), which has its own pledge/reward economics. All PDP listeners (FWSS + Storacha + direct) ≈ 25 TiB actively proven on mainnet today.

FIL price — converts USDFC fees into the FIL they burn at auction (and values block rewards). Lower FIL price → more FIL burned per USDFC of fees.

2 · Fee regime

Preset — quick-loads the slider set for Mainnet v1.2.x (0.5% + 0.1 USDFC sybil burn), v1.3.0 (0.5%, createDataSet fee → SP), or a Redesign starting point. Presets just move the sliders; tweak freely after.

Network fee on settlement — the % taken from each settlement (the “payment fee”, 0.5% today). On FIL rails it’s burned directly; on USDFC/other rails it goes to the auction pool.

createDataSet fee — one-time USDFC fee per new dataset (0.1 in v1.2.x, 0.025 in v1.3.0).

createDataSet fee destination — Burned (into the auction pool → contributes to FIL burn) or To SP (provider revenue, no burn). This one toggle is the v1.2.x → v1.3.0 change and the single biggest driver of fee-burn.

New datasets per quarter @Q2 — datasets created in the first measured quarter; scales with FPV thereafter. Drives createDataSet-fee volume. Simplifying proxy — set it to your real datasets-per-volume ratio.

Proof-fee rate — FIL burned per TiB per proof (PDPVerifier DEFAULT_FEE_PER_TIB, default 0.00023, governable). Proofs per day — proofs per dataset per day (1 on mainnet, ~12 on calibnet). Proof burn = rate × stored TiB × proofs/day × 90.

3 · Token mix & non-FIL surcharge

FIL / USDFC / Other shares — % of FPV settled in each token (auto-normalised to 100%). FIL burns directly; USDFC converts to FIL via the weekly auction; other tokens convert via auction too, at a lower efficiency.

Surcharge on other tokens — extra fee on top of the network fee for non-FIL/non-USDFC tokens, pricing their weaker value accrual.

Other-token auction conversion efficiency — assuming every fee auction eventually closes, the share of an other-token pool that converts to FIL after the Dutch-auction discount/delay (USDFC ≈ par; illiquid tokens clear lower). The shortfall vs USDFC is what the surcharge offsets.

USDFC auction conversion efficiency — how much of the USDFC pool’s value becomes FIL burn at auction (100% = no discount; lower models the Dutch-auction haircut).
